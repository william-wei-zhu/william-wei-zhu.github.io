CEO turnover and cultural change

[Work in Progress. Contact me for research proposal]

Do CEO turnovers affect company culture? In this research project, I analyze the impact of CEO turnovers on employees' perception of company culture using Glassdoor company review data of 1000+ public companies in the United States between 2009 and 2019. I plan to employ text analysis to detect changes in cultural topics of employee reviews during the two years before and after a CEO turnover in each company. To control for confounding factors, I plan to conduct 1-1 matching between companies that experienced CEO turnovers (treatment group) and those that did not (control group).

The project will further explore how factors including incoming CEO background (promoted internally or appointed externally) and company size affect the magnitude and speed of cultural change as a result of CEO turnovers.

Network Visualization of The Office (US) Characters

[Python Code] [Read More]

Recently, I found The Office (US) transcript dataset from Kaggle, which enables to explore the network relationship among The Office characters through the 9 seasons. The following graph shows the network of The Office characters that appear in at least 500 scenes. Each point (node) represents a character. Each line (edge) between two nodes indicates that the two characters have appeared in the same scene. The width of the edge presents the number of times that the two characters shared a scene. The graph applies force-directed graph drawing, which means that characters who have more shared scenes with other characters (greater centrality) are more likely to appear at the center of the graph.

Predicting Employee Attrition

[Python Code]

In this Kaggle project, my goal is to predict employee attrition on IBM HR Analytics dataset (1470 rows, 79 variables). I employed several classification methods including logistic regression, Na√Øve Bayes, Decision Tree, K-nearest neighbors, Random Forest, Radial Support Vector Machines. I also experimented with several ensemble methods including Voting Classifier, Bagged Decision Tree, Adaptive Boosting, Gradient Boosting, and XGBoost.

The following graph shows the relative prediction performance of these methods. XGBoost achieves the highest score of 0.876. In feature importance analysis, I found that income, whether work overtime, stock option level, and levels of environment satisfaction are the most important factors associated with employee attrition.
